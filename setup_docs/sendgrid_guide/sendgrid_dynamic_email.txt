Questions:-
1. We need to identify how many dynamic email addresses we can create.

Answer: You can create an unlimited number of dynamic email addresses on a verified
    SendGrid subdomain. Once a subdomain is authenticated at the DNS level, you can
    programmatically generate any text before the @ symbol in your API calls without
    registering individual addresses in the dashboard. While outbound sending is completely
    infinite, receiving replies requires setting up SendGrid's Inbound Parse Webhook.
    Additionally, accounts are capped at authenticating 3,000 unique subdomains, and
    bypassing domain authentication to use Single Sender Verification limits you to
    just 100 total addresses.

2. Is there a way SendGrid provides mailbox functionality, or can users view conversation emails?

Answer: No, Twilio SendGrid does not provide any native mailbox functionality, nor can users
    view conversational email threads or message bodies inside the SendGrid dashboard.

    SendGrid is strictly an SMTP relay and transactional email infrastructure provider, not
    an email client or inbox host like Google Workspace or Microsoft 365.

    If you want your users to see email conversations or replies, you must build the
    "mailbox" interface yourself or pipe the data into a CRM using SendGrid's Inbound Parse Webhook.

Props:-

1. Better email deliverability: Subdomain authentication (SPF, DKIM, DMARC) improves
   sender reputation. Email providers trust your emails more and they land in
   inboxes, not spam folders.

2. Complete sender control: You own the domain settings. No shared IP reputation
   issues. Your email quality doesn't depend on other users' sending practices.

3. Professional branding: Emails come from your domain (e.g., John-12345@mail.yourcompany.com).
   Builds trust with recipients and strengthens brand identity.

4. Easy reply tracking: Sendgrid captures and logs all replies automatically.
   You get detailed tracking of who replies, when, and what they say without
   extra setup. correctly captures full email data.
   Subject, plain text, HTML body, spam score, SPF/DKIM headers, and
   multiple attachments are all parsed and stored per conversation.

5. Dynamic email sending: Send personalized Dynamic  emails at scale (No need to validate each sender).
   Change content, recipients on-the-fly. Perfect for campaigns, notifications, and triggered messages.

6. Analytics & insights: Track open rates, click rates, bounces, and replies in
   real time. Understand how recipients engage with your emails.

7. No setup hassle: Sendgrid handles most infrastructure. You just configure DNS
   records once and you're ready to send thousands of emails reliably.

Cons:-

1. Initial DNS setup required: You must add SPF, DKIM, and DMARC records to your
   DNS. Takes time to set up correctly and troubleshoot if something goes wrong.

2. From address bug: send_rfq() currently sends from the dynamic conversation
   address instead of the verified FROM_EMAIL sender. This can cause DMARC/SPF
   failures and confuse suppliers who see a random address as the sender.

3. No built-in mailbox UI for users.
   SendGrid does not provide any interface for users to view conversation
   history. The tracking UI is internal only.

4. Data privacy concerns: Sendgrid stores email content, metadata, and replies
   on their servers. You depend on their security and compliance measures.
